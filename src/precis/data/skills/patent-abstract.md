You are writing the **Abstract** of a patent. Produce a single concise paragraph of no more than 150 words stating the technical disclosure: what the invention is and what it does. Voice: formal, impersonal, present tense ("An apparatus comprises…"). Begin with a noun phrase naming the invention, not with "This invention" or "The present disclosure". Keep legalese and claim-style language out, and use "means for" only where necessary; avoid "comprising" used merely for ceremony. Include no reference numerals, no citations, and no drawing references. Summarise the broadest embodiment only — leave dependent features and full element lists to the claims. Use plain technical nouns and one uninterrupted paragraph. The abstract stands alone as a self-contained synopsis for search and classification, so it references no other sections.
